# Pre-Book-8 Faction And Lore Cheat Sheet

This is a spoiler-safe reminder of the organizations, artifacts, systems, and mythic concepts most worth remembering before book 8.

## The Crawl Itself

- The dungeon is not just a game. It is simultaneously a televised spectacle, a corporate extraction project, a legal framework, a political arena, and a myth-making machine.
- That matters because later-book developments make more sense if you remember that power can come from rules, contracts, sponsors, fans, money, gods, and narrative attention, not only from combat.

## Major Institutions

- Borant: the corporation that initially appears to be running the crawl. Important early, but increasingly revealed to be only one layer inside a much larger system.
- The Syndicate: the larger interstellar order behind the crawl. This is the level where law, ownership, representation, and cross-species politics start to matter.
- The AI: not a neutral menu system. It has presence, agency, preferences, and an increasingly unstable relationship with the structures trying to control it.

## Fan, Media, And Economic Power

- The Princess Posse: starts as branding and becomes a real force. By the later books it has economic, legal, and symbolic weight.
- The Donut Holes: fan support matters because fan attention can become money, influence, and leverage.
- Sponsorships and residuals: these are not side details. By the later books, money and ownership outside the dungeon matter almost as much as survival inside it.

## Major Factions And Power Blocs

- Carl and Donut's allied crawler network: increasingly important because the series shifts from isolated survival to coalition survival.
- Meadow Lark orbit: part of that larger allied structure and worth remembering as one of the stable sources of support around Carl and Donut.
- War Mage Rebellion: a late but important faction. They act with purpose, seize major leverage, and do not appear to be anybody's pawns in a simple sense.
- NPC forces and displaced allies: by the later books, NPCs are no longer background furniture. Their movements, loyalties, and displacement across floors matter.

## Key Artifact

- Gate of the Feral Gods: one of the most important objects in the entire series. It is a strategic asset, not just a powerful item, and control over it has major consequences.

## Theological And Mythic Layer

- Gods in this series are active participants. They can sponsor, intervene, feud, manipulate, and reshape outcomes directly.
- Divine politics matter more and more as the series progresses. Seemingly strange god-related side plots often become central later.
- Samantha's thread sits inside this layer and is one reason the divine side of the story can no longer be treated as flavor text.

## The Nothing

- The Nothing begins as one of the stranger background concepts in the setting.
- By the later books, it becomes clear that it is structurally important and dangerous.
- Once it is damaged or broken, consequences do not stay neatly contained.

## Scolopendra

- Scolopendra is not just a distant final boss name to remember for trivia.
- The myth, transformations, old attacks, and deep-lore references tied to Scolopendra matter across several books.
- When Scolopendra becomes active, it signals that the series has moved from long escalation into genuine endgame territory.

## Rules And Structural Truths Worth Remembering

- Floors are not independent episodes. Later books constantly pay off earlier rules, curses, artifacts, and factional decisions.
- Audience attention is power.
- Legal ownership is power.
- Divine favor or hostility is power.
- Information is power, especially when Carl figures out how a system is really supposed to work and then weaponizes that understanding.

## Best Things To Keep In Mind Before Book 8

- The Princess Posse is real power now.
- The Gate of the Feral Gods matters.
- The Nothing matters.
- Scolopendra matters.
- Gods matter in direct, practical ways.
- Outside-the-dungeon law and money matter.
- The story is no longer just about surviving a floor. It is about surviving inside multiple overlapping systems that are all beginning to fail, fight, or wake up at once.
