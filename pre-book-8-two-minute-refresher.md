# Pre-Book-8 Two-Minute Refresher

- Carl and Donut are no longer just surviving floor to floor. By the end of book 7, they are faction leaders, symbols, and political problems for the people running the crawl.
- Mordecai is still the main interpreter of the dungeon's rules and one of their most important anchors.
- Katia is one of the biggest unresolved emotional threads. Her arc matters a lot going into the next book.
- Samantha has gone from bizarre comic chaos to major mythological chaos. She matters.
- Louis matters more than his comic-relief role suggests because he keeps ending up tied to major supernatural and NPC fallout.
- Prepotente is a serious player now, not just an eccentric side character.
- Agatha is still a mystery, and by now it is clear that multiple dangerous groups think she is important.
- Lucia Mar is no longer just "powerful and scary." She is one of the series' biggest unresolved moral and tactical disasters.
- Li Na's situation has changed in a way that matters.
- The Princess Posse is now real power, not just a fan label.
- The Gate of the Feral Gods is one of the most important artifacts in the story, and who controls it matters.
- The War Mage Rebellion and Akuma become important late and fast.
- Justice Light turns the Nothing from background lore into an active problem.
- Scolopendra becoming active is one of the clearest signals that the story is moving into endgame territory.

## One-Paragraph Version

If you only remember one thing before book 8, remember this: the series started as Carl and Donut trying to survive a deadly dungeon, but by the end of book 7 they are trying to hold together allies, followers, and fragile relationships while the crawl turns into a full political, legal, mythological, and factional war. The biggest things to keep in mind are Katia's unresolved fate, Samantha's growing importance, Agatha's mystery, Lucia Mar's unresolved danger, Li Na's altered state, the importance of the Gate of the Feral Gods, the new danger around the Nothing, and Scolopendra's activation.
