# Pre-Book-8 Character Cheat Sheet

This is a spoiler-safe reminder of the characters and groups most worth having fresh in mind before book 8.

## Core Pair

- Carl: the emotional and strategic center of the series. He survives by improvising, reading systems better than their designers expect, and refusing to abandon the people he decides are his responsibility.
- Princess Donut: Carl's partner, top crawler, caster, celebrity, and political symbol. She is funny until she is terrifying, and by this point she is both a real force inside the dungeon and a major public figure outside it.

## Essential Allies

- Mordecai: game guide, manager, teacher, historian, and one of the few people who can reliably explain what is happening without trying to manipulate Carl and Donut for an angle.
- Mongo: Donut's pet and one of the emotional constants of the series. Also frequently a practical battlefield asset.
- Katia: one of Carl and Donut's closest allies for most of the series. Her story is tied to trauma, loyalty, self-doubt, survival, and one of the most painful unresolved outcomes heading into book 8.
- Louis: often the story's comic valve, but repeatedly central to major consequences involving NPCs, Samantha, and larger supernatural fallout.
- Elle McGib: hard-bitten, practical, dangerous, and one of the more reliable members of the extended allied circle.
- Imani: compassionate, steady, and consistently important as part of the Meadow Lark orbit and later allied group.
- Daniel Bautista: loyal, formidable, and especially important through his connection to Katia and the broader ally network.
- Florin: a long-running ally whose connection to Lucia Mar becomes especially important once her situation grows more complicated.
- Prepotente: starts as an oddball and becomes much more than that. He is grief-driven, intense, informed, and unusually important whenever deeper dungeon lore enters the picture.
- Bianca: tied to Prepotente and part of the cluster of threads that make his story more important than it first appears.
- Rosetta: increasingly relevant in the later books and part of the group still worth tracking closely.
- Tipid: another later-series figure who matters because he remains near several key moving parts in the aftermath of Faction Wars.
- Quasar: Carl's lawyer and one of the most useful outside-the-dungeon allies. He is abrasive, but materially helpful.

## High-Priority Unresolved Characters

- Samantha: one of the most deceptively important characters in the series. What began as absurd chaos has become a major thread involving identity, divinity, memory gaps, enemies, and old mythic structures.
- Agatha: the shopping-cart woman and one of the oldest mysteries in the series. She has never fit cleanly into the story's normal categories, and by now it is obvious that other dangerous players think she matters a great deal.
- Lucia Mar: one of the most alarming and unresolved people in the series. She is not just a threat to beat; she is tied to a much uglier, more tragic underlying situation.
- Li Na: once an especially strong ally in the broader crawler network, now someone whose condition and future significance feel much less stable.
- Akuma: leader of the War Mage Rebellion and one of the biggest newer variables introduced late in book 7.

## Important Groups And Factional Figures

- The Princess Posse: no longer just a fandom joke or team label. It is now an actual power bloc with money, influence, legal significance, and symbolic force.
- Donut Holes: part fan group, part political/economic force, and part reminder that audience attention now has real strategic weight.
- War Mage Rebellion: a late-emerging faction that acts decisively and leaves with major leverage.
- Meadow Lark crew: still important as the series increasingly treats survival as collective rather than purely individual.

## Divine And Mythic Figures To Keep Straight

- Scolopendra: the great endgame presence hanging over the series. Not just a final boss name, but part of the deeper mythic structure of the dungeon.
- Emberus: one of the gods whose role matters because divine politics have become direct and practical rather than distant lore.
- Yarilo: important because Samantha's thread and broader divine-family entanglements run through him.
- Eris: another figure whose appearances and associations matter because the divine layer of the story is no longer optional background.
- Theia: central to the deeper questions around Samantha's identity and origin.
- Justice Light: crucial not because of screen time alone, but because of what his actions do to the larger structure of the setting.

## Character Memory Shortcuts

- If you remember Samantha, Agatha, Katia, Lucia Mar, Li Na, Prepotente, and Akuma, you will have most of the character threads that seem most likely to matter going forward.
- If you remember Carl, Donut, and Mordecai as the emotional and strategic core, the rest of the series stays easier to orient.
- If a character once seemed like comic relief, weird flavor, or side chaos, there is a good chance they became structurally important later.
